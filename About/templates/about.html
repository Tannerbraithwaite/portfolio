About Me

I am a father, a science enthusiast, a history lover, a world traveller, a musician, a gym rat, and a software developer who has a passion for life-long education. I enjoy reading, and learning about a large variety of subjects including math, physics, data science, technology, computer sciences, engineering, and history.

I studied at the University of British Columbia from September 2011 to January 2015. I graduated with a degree in physics, and focused on computer science courses outside my core subjects. Some of my course highlights include Computational Physics, Human Computer Interactions, Development in Java, and Python Development for Physics Labs.

During my time at the University I completed an 8 month co-op internship. I worked for the Faculty of Pharmacy, and the Faculty of Land and Food Systems as a Learning Technology Rover. After Graduation I worked as a software developer both in a corporate environment, and as a freelancer.

Outside work, I've always had a passion for travelling, and a desire to see the world. For the past three years I've been living and working in Nanjing, China During my time here I've been able to travel to Indonesia, Thailand, The Philippines, Vietnam, Cambodia, and many places inside China.

I have a wonderful 1 year old son. I became a father in September 2020, and consistently strive to be the type of person I want my son to look up.

My personal hobbies include working out and cooking healthier and higher protein versions of the foods we typically love(ice cream, pizza, pastas etc.). During my weekends and holidays, I enjoy hiking mountains, watching history documentaries, and playing guitar.

I consider myself to be a lifetime student. I find learning to be fulfilling in nature, and a major motivation for everything I do.
